Introduction to Digital Art

Digital art is artwork created using digital technology such as tablets, styluses, and creative software. It allows artists to experiment freely and refine their work in ways traditional media cannot.

Many creators love digital art because it is flexible, efficient, and constantly evolving. With the right tools, anyone can begin creating.

Popular Digital Art Software

• Procreate – A powerful and beginner-friendly iPad drawing app.

• Clip Studio Paint – Widely used for comics, manga, and illustration.

• Adobe Photoshop – Industry-standard software for digital illustration and design.

• Krita – Free, powerful, and very popular among indie and beginner artists.

• GIMP – Good free alternative for photo editing and basic illustration work.

• Corel Painter – Professional painting software with realistic brush simulation.

• Autodesk SketchBook – Simple, beginner-friendly sketching program.

Digital Art Tools

Most digital artists use a graphic tablet or tablet device paired with professional software to simulate real brushes and pens digitally.

Where to Share Your Digital Art

Mainstream Social Media Platforms:

• Instagram – Great for visual portfolios and short reels.
• TikTok – Popular for speedpaints and art process videos.
• Twitter (X) – Widely used by artists for community engagement.
• Bluesky – Growing platform with a strong art community.
• Tumblr – Good for sharing art, sketches, and fandom-style creative work.
• Pinterest – Excellent for inspiration boards and saving visual portfolio pieces.
• YouTube – Ideal for posting speedpaints, tutorials, and art process videos..

Dedicated Art Communities:

• DeviantArt – Long-standing digital art community.
• ArtStation – Professional portfolio platform for concept artists and illustrators.
• Behance – Creative showcase platform by Adobe.
• Pixiv – Popular among anime and manga artists.
• Newgrounds – Creative community for artists and animators.
